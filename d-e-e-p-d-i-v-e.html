LEVEL 2

Artificial Intelligence

Feel free to touch the water~
AI IMAGE GENERATION IS NOT MAGIC, SORRY.

The core idea in AI Image Generation, as you can tell from its name, is Artificial Intelligence (AI) —a branch of computer science that is 60 years of old (Brighterion, 2017).

AI deals with technologies and algorithms that are capable of mimicking human tasks and behavior by simulating intelligence (Townshend, 2022). AI allows machines to recognize speech, make independent decisions, understand human languages, practice visual perception, and many more.

In short, AI is an umbrella term regarding anything that makes computers smart
(Tiwari et al., 2018).
LEVEL 3

Machine Learning

Calm your nerves and dip your legs. You’re ready.

Machine learning (ML) is a subset of AI. It consists of algorithms that execute intelligent tasks by learning directly from data instead of following rules that are pre-programmed by a human developer (Tiwari et al., 2018).

In traditional programming, human developers would code explicit step-by-step rules that tell a computer program how to do something. In ML, the computer program will instead be given tasks, then it will analyze large amounts of data (datasets)—provided by human developers—to find patterns or examples of how its assigned tasks can be accomplished. Over time, this process also referred to as “training,” allows the computer program to learn the best way to achieve its goal (Tiwari et al., 2018).

Basically, ML allows computer programs to be “programmed by example”
(Brighterion, 2017).

FOR MACHINE LEARNING, “DATA IS CHEAP AND  ABUNDANT 
WHILE KNOWLEDGE IS EXPENSIVE AND SCARCE” 
(Tiwari et al., 2018, p.2).
Machine learning allows knowledge to be extracted from data. Going into more detail, “finding patterns or examples” or “training” means that machine learning algorithms (in computer programs) would classify pieces of data either into several features and/or broad categories. These features or categories will help the algorithm extract knowledge and reach its desired outcome (Tiwari et al.,2018; see also Brighterion, 2017).

Furthermore, how machine learning algorithms use datasets can be separated into various approaches , but two approaches generally stand out: Supervised and Unsupervised learning (Tiwari et al., 2018).
LEVEL 4

Machine Learning Approaches

You took a deep breath and dived!

For the supervised learning approach , machine learning algorithms would be trained by feeding it with labeled datasets . Labeled datasets simply mean that a human developer had already properly described and classified each piece of data. When a task is assigned to a supervised ML algorithm, it will use the labeled datasets as a basis to weigh the different features of data until a proper pattern or example appropriate to the task assigned is determined (Alteryx, n.d.).

In contrast, the unsupervised learning approach would provide machine learning algorithms with unlabeled datasets (Alteryx, n.d.). An unsupervised ML algorithm will sort pieces of data into meaningful categories which will help it uncover patterns and relationships—without human intervention—that are appropriate enough to accomplish the task assigned to it (Brighterion, 2017). These categories, however, need to be meaningful only to the algorithm and not humans, as such incomprehensible categories to humans, like the “banana-ness” of a banana , may be made.

SUPERVISED LEARNING IS LIKE YOUR CRUSH,

IT NEEDS LABELS AND HUMAN (YOU) INTERVENTION .

Supervised learning is used for machine learning tasks that require precise outputs. For example, a machine learning algorithm can predict when a plane is about to leave an airport, by being trained on a human-written dataset. The said dataset can include information about peak hours at the airport and weather types that can affect flight conditions (Alteryx, n.d.).

Unsupervised learning, on the other hand, is mostly used for generative AI. (Alteryx, n.d.).

With all these mechanisms in mind, machine learning technology is used in situational problems wherein there are no perfect or exact answers.

UNLIKE YOUR PARENT’S EXPECTATIONS, MACHINE

LEARNING IS SATISFIED WITH JUST BEING GOOD ENOUGH.

The essence of ML, then, is to make computers that can provide guesses that are “good enough” to be useful in certain scenarios (Tiwari et al., 2018). This led to numerous applications of ML that have allowed it to shape our society today in immeasurably more ways than one.

    Neural Network in Machine Learning

    You stare into the abyss that awaits you...you should keep swimming.
    "Wax on, wax off…we start with the most basic." (Wax on, Wax off, 1984)

    To start discussing generative AI, the most basic concept behind it should be first discussed, namely the Neural Network. Simply put, Neural Network is a concept in AI that corresponds to technologies inspired by the structure of the human brain.


    Visually shown as a web-like connection of nodes, neural networks are composed of interconnected elements with each acting as artificial neurons (Brighterion, 2017).

    LEVEL 7

    Deep Learning

    You’re having deep thoughts about this one.
    If the concept of a simple neural network shown above is multiplied into an interconnected web of thousands of millions of artificial neurons, then stacked into multiple layers, it will result in a technology called Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) (Vadapalli, 2020). ANN technology is the heart of a machine learning technique called Deep Learning (IBM Cloud Education, 2020a).

    Deep learning is a subset of machine learning. It is a type of ML technique that represents human intelligence as layers of neural networks (Vadapalli, 2020).


    Once fed with information from unlabeled datasets,  these layers of neural networks classify information as “a nested hierarchy of concepts, with each concept defined in relation to simpler concepts” (Tiwari et al., 2018, p.1) These concepts along with their relationship to each other are visually represented as interconnected artificial neurons (Vadapalli, 2020).

    Within an ANN, the ML algorithm computes or guesses abstract representations that are “ good enough ” by using data from less abstract information in other layers (Tiwari et al., 2018). In a sense, the layers of ANN basically act as a specific threshold to which artificial neurons are activated and given a concept or more correctly, a mathematical value (Vadapalli, 2020).

    Therefore, deep learning, in a nutshell, is the process of ML algorithms using hierarchical layers that contain a representation of concepts to perform pattern recognition (Brighterion, 2017). The ability for pattern recognition is then applied to machines, resulting in technologies like computer vision, speech recognition, facial recognition, and image recognition (Townshend, 2022).

    LEVEL 9

    Natural Language Processing

    You’re getting closer to the end.

    Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a topic in artificial intelligence that focuses on machines or algorithms that are designed to understand natural language as voice or text data. “Understanding” in NLP is more than just knowledge of the word’s meaning but it also includes understanding elements such as the mood and intent of the speaker, even sarcasm (IBM Cloud Education, 2020b).

    Theory: In I, Robot (2004), Sonny the robot was secretly implemented
    with  NLP to respond to Will Smith’s rhetorical questions with great sarcasm.

    There are various ways to achieve NLP. For generative AI, NLP requires a combination of deep learning algorithms and a “rule-based model of human language,” referred to as computational linguistics. Together, DL algorithms and computational linguistics can mimic how the human mind understands the arbitrariness of natural languages through both written and spoken words (IBM Cloud Education, 2020b).

    The field of Artificial Intelligence was born in 1956 when computer scientists and mathematicians held a six-week research workshop at Dartmouth College regarding “machines that can think” (Moor, 2006). 9 years after, an algorithm similar to the idea of deep learning was introduced by mathematicians Ivakhnenko and Lapa to analyze algebraic concepts (Tiwari et al., 2018).

    Deep learning used to suffer from barriers—such as the lack of large datasets and strong computing power—thdat block its fullest potential. However—decades after its inception—numerous leaps and bounds in various technologies made it possible to breach the said barriers (Marr, 2021).

    •     First , the Internet meant that a massive amount of content is now available for distribution and consumption at a single converged digital space—a perfect location to create a large dataset (McCorduck, 2004).
    •     Second , the wide availability of graphics-processing hardware such as GPUs gave access to computing power that is both cheaper and more powerful than its predecessors (Brighterion, 2017).
    •      Finally , within the field of AI itself, algorithmic advances result in much more efficient machine learning (Tiwari et al., 2018). Specifically for generative AI, these advances come in the form of pattern recognition technology (from DL algorithms) and Natural Language Processing.

    In 2015, AI technology exploded and reached a point of no return. Generative AI made automated image captions possible, DL algorithms were able to automatically label images and with the help of NLP, output those labels as natural languages—in simpler terms, provide images with automatic English captions. This image-to-text technology was reversed the following year to generate entirely new images just from a few words (text-to-image generation) (Vox, 2022).

    Eventually, these developments led to a domino effect wherein modern generative AI in 2022 could create novel images that are technically indiscernible to the style that it aims to mimic, from photorealism to cartoons—in other words, modern AI Image Generation (Vox, 2022b).

    LEVEL 11

    Generative AI

    As Nietzsche puts it, “if you stare into the abyss, the abyss tells
    you about AI”... right?

    Generative AI models power AI Image Generation. They refer to complex deep learning algorithms that understand the structure of an unlabeled dataset, such as pictures with captions, and create completely original products—with the goal of making them as close as possible to the “real” thing (Generative AI Models, 2022).

    To understand its dataset structure, generative models use a combination of limited supervised learning and unsupervised learning, referred to as semi-supervised learning (Generative AI Models, 2022). In comparison to supervised and unsupervised learning, semi-supervised learning is a machine learning approach that extracts knowledge from datasets by determining features on pieces of data (limited supervised) and then classifying them into meaningful categories (unsupervised).

    For a deeper understanding, generative models can be compared to machine learning algorithms that only use supervised learning, these can be referred to as discriminative AI models . Discriminative models are not used for AI generation but for machine learning prediction. They are provided with hand-coded rules by human developers and these rules refer to existing data points with their relationship to each other already pre-established. The discriminative model will analyze new information and predict a label for it using its hand-coded rules as the basis (Generative AI Models, 2022).

    For example, a discriminative AI model is taught that bananas are yellow and apples are red.
    If presented with a yellow balloon, the model will return an answer that tells that the yellow balloon is not an apple but something that is probably similar to a banana.

    In short, a discriminative model contains information about bananas and apples but it does not exactly know what makes a banana or what an apple is.

    In contrast, generative AI models try to answer “what makes a banana?” using semi-supervised learning. Going back to our example, a generative AI model is given a dataset with a specific label that consists of a picture of a banana with an image caption (limited supervised learning), then it tries to determine the possible features of the banana using deep learning (unsupervised learning).

    The generative model does not only know that a banana is yellow, but it also knows its shape, texture, other incomprehensible characteristics such as “ banana-ness ,” and the relationship of Bananas with other concepts. Therefore, if the generative model truly knows “what makes a banana” from an image with a caption, then it follows that it can recreate an entirely new image of a banana.

    LEVEL 12

    AI Image Generation

    You have arrived.

    By now, everything under the belt of AI to the specifics of Text-to-Image generation had been discussed, all that is left is the technology itself.

    Generative AI models built with deep learning and Natural Language Processing would result in a breakthrough technology called Transformer-based models. The most prominent example of a transformer-based model is GPT-3 (Generative Pre-Trained 3), GPT-3 is the third generation of a generative AI that was built by a major AI research laboratory and company called OpenAI (Generative AI Models, 2022).

    Using GPT-3 along with other various AI technologies, OpenAI was able to launch an AI Image Generator called DALL-E in January 2021 and its upgrade, DALL-E 2, the following year. OpenAI claims that DALL-E 2 can “create realistic images and art from a description in natural language” (DALL-E 2, n.d.).

    It is to be noted that there are other variations of generative AI models such as Generative Adversarial Networks or GANs. Since GANs and transformer-based models have the same output—in this context as AI-generated images—GANs would no longer be discussed in this guide (Generative AI Models, 2022).

    Prior to deep learning, DALL-E first requires a gargantuan and diverse dataset for training. OpenAI solves this problem by collecting hundreds of millions of alt text and their corresponding images all over the Web. Alt text refers to text descriptions that people add when uploading images on the web for the purpose of web accessibility and allowing it to be easily organized by search engines (i.e., search engine indexation). With this, OpenAI was able to satisfy the requirements of DALL-E without having the need to manually tag images with captions and create hand-coded rules, hence a dataset with very minimal labels (limited supervised learning) (Vox, 2022a).

    DALL-E then proceeds to feed on its massive dataset to allow its transformer-based model to generate images. The transformer-based model can generally be separated into two parts: encoder and decoder (Generative AI Models, 2022).
    The encoder is what feeds onto the input/dataset (Generative AI Models, 2022). It tries to extract all the features present in an image and its corresponding text description, and convert them into meaningful categories to pass to the decoder.

    What the “extract all the features” and “convert them into meaningful categories” actually mean is that the encoder determines “features” of the image (as pixel values) and converts them as vectors or “meaningful categories” to separate them from one another in a mathematical space—this concept is known as latent space (Vox, 2022a). Then using these categories, the encoder matches the images to their text descriptions a thousand times until it figures out a method that is “good enough”—this is what is referred to as deep learning (Generative AI Models, 2022); see also (Vox, 2022a).

    DALL-E is not only required to understand what makes a banana but it is also tasked to determine what makes other things and how are they different or similar from bananas. In other words, generative AI models, in general, do not only define what an object is but also define its relationship with other objects.

    In reality, DALL-E creates at least 500 categories for each feature (Vox, 2022a), it follows that humans do not have all the words to describe these, resulting in incomprehensible regions of information like “banana-ness” amongst many, many other things.

    Once the encoder creates the meaningful categories, the decoder “receives” them to create an output of generated novel images. The decoder achieves this through the use of a mechanism called Diffusion that “picks” a mathematical point/value in the latent space, then translates it to an image pixel. The combination of image pixels first starts out as noise and then eventually becomes a “proper” image after a series of iterations (Generative AI Models, 2022; see also Vox, 2022a).

    Because of the randomness of diffusion, it will never return the same image from the same set of words, also referred to as prompts (Vox, 2022a).

    Prompting, prompt engineering, or prompt craft is the terminology used for the art of writing prompts for an AI Image Generator (Vox, 2022a).
